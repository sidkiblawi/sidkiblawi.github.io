Ranking 100 records against each other is a losing game. Grouping them isn't.
What follows is my personal top 100, sorted not by genre but by vibes. A confessional folk record and a bedroom-recorded rap album can be
closer siblings than two things that both happen to have guitars.

Some of my criteria:

- I went about this list in one way. If I were to start a record collection and I had a 100 album budget. This is what I'd grab.
- Few skips. The albums I tend to listen to beginning to end, not just a few tracks here and there.
- While I’ve focused on diversity (tried to limit albums from same artist to just 2), the list also includes artists with troubled legacies; I don't always believe in separating the art from the artist, but I can't deny that these remain some influential records.
- Didn't include any live albums. No Cornell 77.
